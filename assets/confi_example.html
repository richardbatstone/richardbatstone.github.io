1.           Confidential Information.

1.1         Definition of Confidential Information. The parties agree that all code, know-how, business, technical and financial information it obtains (“Receiving Party”) from the disclosing party (“Disclosing Party”) constitute the confidential property of the Disclosing Party (“Confidential Information”), provided that it is identified as confidential at the time of disclosure or should be reasonably known by the Receiving Party to be Confidential Information due to the nature of the information disclosed. Any system, documentation or technical information provided by Supplier and performance information relating to the System shall be deemed Confidential Information of Supplier without any marking or further designation. Customer Data shall be deemed Confidential Information of the Customer without any marking or further designation.

1.2         Non-Disclosure. Except as expressly authorised herein, the Receiving Party will: (i) hold in confidence and not use or disclose any Confidential Information; and (ii) not use any Confidential Information other than for the purpose for which it was provided. Subject to clause 1.3, the Receiving Party’s nondisclosure obligation shall not apply to information which the Receiving Party can document: (i) was rightfully in its possession or known to it prior to receipt of the Confidential Information; (ii) is or has become public knowledge through no fault of the Receiving Party; (iii) is rightfully obtained by the Receiving Party from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation; (iv) is independently developed by employees of the Receiving Party who had no access to such information; or (v) is required to be disclosed pursuant to a regulation, law or court order. The Receiving Party acknowledges that disclosure of Confidential Information would cause substantial harm for which damages alone would not be a sufficient remedy, and therefore that upon any such disclosure by the Receiving Party the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to appropriate equitable relief in addition to whatever other remedies it might have at law.

1.3        Non-disclosure of Client Data. In no event may Supplier or Supplier’s Personnel disclose any Customer Data that relates to any Client of the Customer or which relates to the respective officers, employees, agents, contractors, sub-contractors or counterparties of any Client of the Customer (“Client Data”), at any time or for any reason other than: (i) to the Customer; or (ii) in accordance with the prior written consent of Customer.
